User Trailing

Creative Coding

We followed the user trialling technique, which is the observation of people using a product and collection of comments from people who have used a product. Additionally, we implemented the creative technique “thought bubble”. We made 5 different research questions, and at the end of the trial, our goal was to answer them. \n We presented our results in a couple different ways, by having 5 different research questions, it gave us the freedom to present our results in a plethora of different ways. Below I will state the research questions with the following presentation techniques. \n 1)What do participants in user trialling actually do when using Rijksstudio? Flowchart \n What usability problems are users facing? What causes these problems? Usability problem table \n How does the app perform on the 3 criterias of usability (effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction)? Time taken table for tasks \n Rijksmuseum offers both a website and a mobile app, does the user experience translate seamlessly and well from web to app version? Rating -2 to 2 (-2 - very bad, 2 - very good): How does the user flow change with the use of different devices? Flowchart of tasks
